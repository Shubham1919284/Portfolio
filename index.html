Black Friday Purchase Prediction

Python · XGBoost · Scikit-Learn · Streamlit · Kaggle Dataset

1. Why This Project Matters

This project demonstrates a complete Machine Learning regression workflow—from exploratory data analysis and feature engineering to model benchmarking, deployment, and real-time prediction using a large-scale retail dataset.

2. Business Problem

During massive retail events like Black Friday, retailers struggle to accurately forecast individual customer spend. Without precise predictive models, inventory management suffers and marketing campaigns waste budget on low-converting users.

3. Business Impact

Predicting purchase amounts allows for proactive Inventory Planning and Marketing Optimization. By understanding expected customer spend, businesses can allocate stock efficiently and tailor promotions to high-value segments, directly increasing ROI.

5. Engineering Pipeline

Dataset → Exploratory Data Analysis → Data Cleaning → Feature Engineering → Encoding & Scaling → Model Training → Model Evaluation → Deployment

6. Engineering Decisions

Why XGBoost?

Selected for its superior generalization and lower prediction error on structured tabular data, efficiently handling sparse categorical matrices.

Why StandardScaler?

The same scaler fitted during training is strictly reused during inference to maintain feature consistency and prevent prediction drift in production.

Why Model Comparison?

Multiple regression architectures were benchmarked to empirically validate the bias-variance trade-off before committing to a final deployment artifact.

7. Technical Evaluation

10. Lessons Learned

  • Feature Engineering influenced overall R² performance significantly more than hyperparameter tuning across algorithms.
  • Tree-based ensembles drastically outperformed linear regression by naturally mapping non-linear purchasing behaviors.
  • Product category constraints provided stronger signal variance than customer demographic data.

Telecom Customer Churn Analysis

Python · Pandas · Matplotlib · Seaborn

1. Why This Project Matters

This project demonstrates a rigorous analytical engineering approach to diagnosing customer retention issues. By systematically cleaning, transforming, and visualizing telecom data, raw historical records are converted into actionable business intelligence.

2. Business Problem

Telecom providers lose millions annually to customer churn. Without a data-driven understanding of exactly why and when customers leave, retention budgets are wasted on broad strategies rather than targeted, high-ROI interventions.

3. Business Impact

Pinpointing the exact drivers of churn allows the business to overhaul pricing tiers, modify contract incentives, and deploy targeted retention campaigns, directly protecting recurring subscription revenue.

4. Dataset Overview

7,043
Dataset Size
21
Features
Analytical
Project Type
Churn Status
Target Variable

Data Source: Telco Customer Churn Kaggle Dataset

5. Analytical Workflow

Dataset → Data Cleaning → Exploratory Data Analysis → Statistical Analysis → Feature Selection → Visualization → Business Recommendations

6. Engineering Decisions

Why prioritize EDA?

Extensive exploratory data analysis was prioritized to validate feature integrity and ensure that missing values (e.g., TotalCharges) were systematically resolved before generating insights.

Why statistical analysis?

Applied distribution mapping to distinguish actual statistical significance from random variance when evaluating customer departure rates.

Why focus on contracts?

Contract type, tenure, and service usage were isolated as the primary focus because variance metrics indicated they held the highest signal density for churn prediction.

7. Key Analytical Findings

  • Month-to-Month customers showed significantly higher churn rates compared to annual subscribers.
  • Fiber Optic customers churned more frequently than DSL users, indicating potential service quality or pricing issues.
  • Long-tenure customers were exponentially more likely to stay, demonstrating strong baseline loyalty after the first year.

10. Lessons Learned

  • Demographic variables (e.g., gender) exhibited near-zero predictive correlation compared to financial account parameters.
  • High monthly charges strongly correlate with churn only when not offset by multi-year contract incentives.
  • Clean data visualization proved more effective at communicating risk to non-technical stakeholders than raw statistical outputs.

11. Engineering Roadmap

This foundational analysis directly informs the feature selection process for building a future predictive churn model. Future enhancements include:

  • Training a Logistic Regression baseline model
  • Benchmarking against tree-based classifiers (Random Forest, XGBoost, LightGBM)
  • Deploying a real-time churn risk evaluation API

Key Engineering Takeaways

  • Data cleaning (coercing data types) was the most critical step for correlation accuracy.
  • Financial incentives completely dominated demographic factors in churn prediction.
  • Clear visual storytelling proved essential for translating statistical significance into business action.
